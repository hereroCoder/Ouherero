History

The Herero people are believed to have migrated from East Africa into present-day Namibia around the 16th century. As a pastoralist community, they brought with them large herds of cattle, sheep, and goats.

OvaHerero people migrated from Namibia to neighbouring countries like Botswana and South Africa during the genocide in 1904. Some returned back to Namibia later on.Most populations of Herero people are currently found in Botswana, Namibia and Angola
